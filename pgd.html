Post Graduate Diploma in Theology (PGDT)

Veenstra Theology Seminary (VTS) in conjunction with University of Jos to which she is affiliated, offers a 1 year full time course leading to a Post Graduate Diploma in Theology (PGDT). Similar version of the PGDT is also available through a part time program over one and half (1.5) years.
The PGDT is designed to act as a bridge between previous degree level studies of different kinds and theological study at Master Level.
Other versions of the PGDT are obtainable depending on both the nature of the previous study (whether in Theology or related discipline or other academic disciplines) and the nature of further study at Master’s level for which the PGDT is intended as preparation.
It is also possible for students to take the PGDT without going on to the Masters Studies. It can still be used to pursue the Masters Degree subsequently either at VTS or another Seminary. The difference between the full time and part time programs shall be in the intensity of teaching the part time courses and in the separation of written assignments, which are to be done by the end of the December following the teaching and examination of each course. Written assignment is required to encourage research and writings. More to this, lecturers are also encourage to provide hand-outs for part time students.
Students will be required to buy at least one text book per course at registration. The part time course is organized on the basis of two modules per session; each four weeks long, with a one week gap between modules i.e. a total of nine weeks long in a session. The session will fall within the long vacation of each academic year. The part time course will be done in three modules over one and a half years.
The pass mark for all PGDT courses will be 50%. A student who does not attain this must retake a compulsory course; failed elective course may either be retaken or an equivalent course taken in place. No re-sit examinations are allowed according to the regulations of the University of Jos. Marks for failed courses will be recorded on the student’s transcript, but will not be considered in the calculation of the final Grade Point Average (GPA).
