About VTS

Brief History

VTS is the official Seminary of the Christian Reformed Church Nigeria (CRC-N). It was first established in 1960 at Lupwe at the suburb of Takum now in Ussa Local Government, Taraba State, offering a lower level Pre-Seminary program in the English Language to prepare candidates for further theological studies at the Theological College of Northern Nigeria (TCNN) in Bukuru, near Jos.

Most of those who completed this training are actively involved in the church and government. In 1976, the Seminary started training pastors in the Hausa Language, while shortly thereafter, in 1981, the school was moved from Lupwe to Wukari.

Mission & Vission

Mission Statement

VTS exists for the training and equipping the church and society leaders with the neccessary knowledge and skills for Pastoral Ministry and to improve the general education of the leaders and their family members in keeping with the contemporary educational needs of the church in Nigeria and beyond.

Vission Statement

VTS seeks to prepare students and families with solid foundations for the highest spiritual and academic standards in the church and the society.

Aim and Objectives

Aim

VTS seeks to train, equip and prepare people for positions of leadership in the church and society at large through its carefully designed programs.

Objectives

The objective of these programs is to provide students with the courses and skills needed in pastoral ministry, though it also serves to train people for related leadership positions.
VTS is also committed to the education of the wives who accompany their husbands to school. Presently, the Seminary offers certificate course at the women's school to improve the general level of education of the wives so that they can keep pace with the increasing level of education of their husbands.

Accreditation

VTS has been affiliated to the University of Jos since 2002. It is recognized by all TEKAN Churches and other denominations as an excellent institution for training future ministers, evangelists and teachers both academically and spiritually. VTS is also approved by Taraba State Teaching Service Board, Jalingo.



Doctrinal Believe

‐VTS is reformed in its Doctrinal belief, i.e. the ecumenical creeds. These ecumenical creeds are: the Apostle's Creed, the Nicene Creed, and the Athanasian Creed.
‐The Holy Bible is the foundation of the church, the infallible word of God and without error.
‐The unity of God the father, the son and the Holy Spirit; the incarnate son of God, his death and resurrection and the total depravity of man through Adam's fall.
